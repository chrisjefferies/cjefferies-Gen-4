Quick Philosophy Point: Responsive Design

The State News' approach to web design was primarily desktop-down, starting with the computer interface, and removing site functionality as the device size and capabilities decrease. This is reflected in their device-based pixel width selections in the wireframe design.

Most modern web designers tend toward mobile-up design, packing as much functionality as possible into the broadest array of devices, and then adapt to larger screens for a cohesive, unified experience for the end user. While I tend toward the latter in many cases, I have no problem adapting to another design philosophy in a team environment. I also advocate a more dynamic approach to web development, where responsive breaks are based around the html elements, rather than the spectrum of possible end-user devices, creating a simpler, more cross-compatible, and cohesive site.
