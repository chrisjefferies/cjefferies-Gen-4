An eclectic collection of illustration work, this project features a variety of work in both paper and graphite, and Wacom tablet and Photoshop. I don't consider myself a practicing artist or illustrator. However, I think there are enough important overlaps in skill application that their placement here is warranted. In addition, they demonstrate an attention to form, color and other principles that are important for all design disciplines.

Grism the Angry Viking

A photorealistic character study series. Traditional and digital rendering.

Ink Interpretation of Raphael's St. Michael (ca. 1505)

Study of the renaissance masterpiece with a focus on interpreting the painterly light and shadow marks with line. Micron Pens on Stonehenge Lithography paper.

Trident 120

A digitally rendered comic book in modern big two style. A military action-thriller, Trident 120 is self-published and currently in it's second printing. The ongoing story can be followed in my experiment in creative, jQuery-based, parallax-scrolling webcomics at trident120.com.

The Spirit of the Spartan

My first published work in comics. The MSU Alumni Association published the spirit as part of their 2013 homecoming week membership drive. I was also asked to represent the organization at several events, including ArtPrize and An Evening with Geoff Johns.

Beyond the modest success and positive reception, I was happy with resolution of the piece, as much about comics as a medium as it is about what being a spartan means to me. I should note the prose and some of the visual were an homage to Frank Miller's The Spirit (2008), a little-seen film adaptation of comics legend Will Eisner's iconic work.

Steve Boughton at the Alumni Association later adapted the piece into an animated video.
